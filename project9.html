Flap Design for AADI-2004 Agricultural Aircraft

Designed and integrated a single-slotted Fowler flap extension-retraction system for the AADI-2004 agricultural aircraft to enhance short takeoff and landing (STOL) capabilities while ensuring compliance with airworthiness standards.

Project Overview

This project focused on developing a complete flap mechanism for the AADI-2004 agricultural aircraft, originally designed in AERO 3002. The aircraft required enhanced high-lift capabilities for STOL operations in agricultural environments, including frequent takeoff and landing cycles from untarred farmland strips. The project integrated structural, mechanical, electrical, and avionics interfaces to create a reliable flap system.

Objectives

  • Design a flap extension-retraction system deployable up to 40 degrees
  • Enhance STOL performance for agricultural operations
  • Ensure compliance with CAR/FAR 23 airworthiness standards
  • Integrate mechanical, electrical, and avionics systems
  • Conduct comprehensive safety analysis including Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA)
  • Achieve 23% reduction in stall speed compared to clean configuration

Design Approach

The project evaluated four flap concepts: split flap, single-slotted flap (hydraulic), double-slotted flap, and single-slotted Fowler flap (electric). Through weighted trade study analysis, the single-slotted Fowler flap with electric jackscrew actuation was selected as the optimal solution, offering the best balance of aerodynamic performance, structural integration, and reliability in agricultural environments.

Key Features

  • Single-Slotted Fowler Flap: Combines deflection with aft translation for maximum lift enhancement
  • Electric Jackscrew Actuation: DC motor-driven system with torque tubes and right-angle gearboxes
  • Synchronized Operation: Mechanically linked system ensuring symmetric flap deployment on both wings
  • Track and Carriage System: Ball nut and ballscrew mechanism for precise linear displacement
  • Cockpit Integration: Flap lever control with G3X display for visual feedback
  • Safety Systems: Limit switches, circuit breakers, and comprehensive FMEA analysis
  • Environmental Protection: Sealed mechanisms resistant to dust, moisture, and agricultural chemicals

Work Distribution

  • Mechanical Design: Flap geometry, track/carriage system, hinge layout, torque-tube routing, gearbox placement, and structural interfaces
  • Power and Actuation Systems: Actuator torque/power sizing, aerodynamic analysis, and torque transmission evaluation
  • Control and Avionics Integration: Human-machine interface, electrical architecture, and flap-position sensing
  • Systems Safety and Certification: CAR/FAR 23 compliance, redundancy strategies, and Failure Modes and Effects Analysis

Methods

The design process included comprehensive concept development and selection, detailed system requirements establishment, aerodynamic analysis for lift coefficient optimization, structural analysis for component sizing, and complete Failure Modes and Effects Analysis. The project utilized CAD modeling, aerodynamic simulations, and detailed trade studies to ensure optimal performance and safety.

Tools Used

CATIA for 3D CAD modeling and structural analysis, aerodynamic analysis software for lift coefficient calculations, electrical system design tools, and FMEA analysis frameworks.

Outcomes

Successfully designed and integrated a single-slotted Fowler flap system that achieves a maximum lift coefficient of approximately 3.11, providing a 23% reduction in stall speed. The electric actuation system offers superior reliability in agricultural environments compared to hydraulic alternatives, with comprehensive safety features and full compliance with airworthiness standards. The design maintains accessibility for maintenance while providing the STOL performance required for agricultural operations.
