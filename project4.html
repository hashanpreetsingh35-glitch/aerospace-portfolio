Supersonic Flow Over a 15 Degree Wedge

Performed a steady 2D inviscid CFD analysis of Mach 2 flow over a 15° wedge, focusing on oblique shock formation, pressure rise, and grid independence.

Methods

Implemented 2D inviscid Euler equations for supersonic flow simulation over a 15° wedge. Conducted grid independence studies with multiple mesh resolutions to ensure solution accuracy. Analyzed oblique shock formation, validated theoretical shock angles, and examined pressure distribution across the flow field.

Tools Used

ANSYS Fluent for CFD setup, meshing, and simulation execution.

Outcome

Successfully validated CFD predictions against theoretical oblique shock relations, achieving accurate shock angle calculation (~40.3°) and detailed pressure contour analysis. Demonstrated proficiency in supersonic aerodynamics and computational fluid dynamics.
