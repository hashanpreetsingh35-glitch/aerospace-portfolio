Retractable Landing Gear Design for Light Sport Aircraft

Designed and developed a hybrid actuated landing gear system for a Light Sport Aircraft (LSA) to enhance aerodynamic performance, reduce drag, and improve overall efficiency while adhering to Canadian Aviation Regulations.

Project Overview

This project focused on developing a retractable hybrid landing gear system for a Light Sport Aircraft, replacing the traditional fixed landing gear. The tricycle configuration ensures stability and ease of maintenance, incorporating oleo struts for shock absorption, hydraulic disc brakes for deceleration, and electric and hydraulic actuation mechanisms for reliable gear deployment and retraction.

Objectives

  • Design an actuated landing gear system that enhances aircraft performance
  • Reduce aerodynamic drag by retracting main landing gear into the aircraft fuselage without bay doors
  • Comply with Canadian Aviation Regulations (CARs) and safety standards
  • Balance system complexity with operational efficiency and weight considerations
  • Maintain high safety standards with redundant mechanisms and emergency systems

Design Approach

The project evaluated three primary design concepts before selecting a hybrid tricycle configuration. The selected design combines electric actuation for the nose landing gear (powered by the aircraft engine) with hydraulic actuation for the main landing gear. This hybrid approach achieves an optimal balance between innovation and practicality, minimizing drag and weight while maintaining reliability.

Key Features

  • Hybrid Tricycle Configuration: Combines electric and hydraulic actuation mechanisms
  • Nose Landing Gear (NLG): Electric actuation with bay door protection
  • Main Landing Gear (MLG): Hydraulic actuation with streamlined gear doors
  • Shock Absorption: Oleo struts for efficient force damping during landing
  • Braking System: Hydraulic disc brakes for effective deceleration
  • Safety Mechanisms: Redundant uplocks and downlocks, manual hand-crank emergency deployment
  • Real-time Monitoring: Cockpit interface for control and status monitoring

Methods

The design process included detailed concept development and selection, creating a Detailed Set of System Requirements (SRD) aligned with Canadian Aviation Regulations and customer specifications. Structural analysis was performed to evaluate load capacity, stress distribution, and component durability. Aerodynamic analysis verified the drag reduction benefits of the retractable system. Interface design ensured seamless integration with the existing aircraft systems.

Tools Used

CATIA for 3D CAD modeling and component design, structural finite element analysis for stress evaluation, hydraulic and electrical system design software, and regulatory compliance documentation tools.

Outcomes

Successfully designed a hybrid retractable landing gear system that meets all regulatory requirements and customer specifications. The design adds approximately 25-45 lbs to the aircraft while significantly reducing aerodynamic drag during cruise. The hybrid configuration provides cost-effective innovation without compromising reliability or safety, resulting in improved aircraft performance and operational efficiency. The system was fully documented with detailed technical specifications, assembly drawings, and regulatory compliance analysis.
